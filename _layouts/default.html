Joyce Ovalle

The University of Texas at Austin

  • CV

I am a PhD Candidate at UT Austin’s LBJ School of Public Affairs. My research interests are on the role of civil society organizations, particularly environmental nonprofits and their funders, in shaping public policies. My professional journey includes roles at the Urban Institute's Center on Nonprofits and Philanthropy, research positions at Texas A&M University, and contributions to the public sector through service with the U.S. Department of Energy. I am a proud first-generation alumnus of Texas Public Schools.

  • Ovalle, J., Bixler, R. P., Ma, J., How do organizational characteristics shape environmental philanthropy in Texas? A network science approach (Under review with Nonprofit Policy Forum)

    • This study uses statewide survey data (n=114) and exponential random graph modeling (ERGM) to investigate the organizational and network factors impacting foundation grant-getting for environmental nonprofits in Texas. In line with elitism perspectives, we observe the likelihood of foundation grants going to those nonprofits who are already receiving financial grants (known in network analysis as preferential attachment). We also find that financial resources were a statistically significant predictor as the higher a nonprofit’s level of resources the more likely they are to receive a foundation grant. However, from a pluralist perspective, we also observe that local geography was meaningful since environmental nonprofits conducting work in areas with higher environmental risks were more likely to receive foundation funding.
